Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about Microsoft 365 and Office 2021.

What is Microsoft 365?

Microsoft 365 is a subscription service that ensures you always have the most up-to-date modern productivity tools from Microsoft. There are Microsoft 365 plans for home and personal use, as well as for small and midsized businesses, large enterprises, schools, and non-profits.

What's the difference between Office 2021 and Microsoft 365?

Microsoft 365 is a subscription service that comes with premium Office apps, such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. It also includes OneDrive storage, advanced security, and more. Office 2021 is sold as a one-time purchase and includes classic apps such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint for use on a single PC or Mac.

Can I use Microsoft 365 or Office 2021 on multiple devices?

Microsoft 365 can be installed across multiple devices, including PCs, Macs, tablets, and smartphones. The number of devices you can use depends on your plan. Office 2021 is a one-time purchase for use on a single device.

Is internet access required for Microsoft 365?

You do not need to be connected to the internet to use Office apps, such as Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, because these apps are fully installed on your computer. However, an internet connection is required to install and activate all the latest releases of Office suites and all Microsoft 365 subscription plans.

How do I install Microsoft 365 or Office 2021?

For installation instructions, visit our installation guide to get step-by-step directions on how to download and install Microsoft 365 or Office 2021 on your device.

What happens when my Microsoft 365 subscription ends?

If you cancel your subscription or it expires, you can still access and download all your files by signing in to OneDrive directly using the Microsoft account you used to set up Microsoft 365. You’ll lose the additional storage that comes with the subscription, so you’ll need to save your files elsewhere or buy more OneDrive storage.
